This line graph depicts changes in the sex ratio of newborns from 1950 to 2023 for the British Virgin Islands and Bonaire Sint Eustatius and Saba.       For Bonaire Sint Eustatius and Saba, the ratio oscillates around 102%.       It remained stable between 1950 and 1958, however reaching a valley value of 97 in 1960.       Then it continued to rise until 1968, and suddenly fell to 100 in 1977.       It exhibits some cyclical characteristics afterward as before, reaching a peak in 1992 at 115.5, then a valley value in 2000.       Then it resumed to rise, and experienced a big jump in 2019 followed by a sudden increase to 118.2 in 2022.       For the British Virgin Islands, it oscillates slightly around 105, and is relatively stable overall;       it is also somewhat cyclical, with a very small value of 95 around 1990, and a very large value of 112 around 2005.       In comparison, both oscillate, and both exhibit some cyclicality, but the British Virgin Islands are more stable and the Bonaire Sint Eustatius and Saba are more cyclical.
