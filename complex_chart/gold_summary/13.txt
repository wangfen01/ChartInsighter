This line chart depicts the monthly rent values and the monthly equivalent rent values of primary residences in the U.S. from January 1984 to February 2015. Rent values peaked at about 6.6 around 1986, followed by stable fluctuations of above 4 between 1987 and 1988. Then the values gradually rose with fluctuations until 1990 at 6.2. And it suddenly fell to 2.8 in 1991. Then it resumed fluctuating in the next 10 years. Another peak occurred around 2002, with a high point around 4.5, then it fell to 2 in 2004 followed by another peak at 4.3 in 2007. Afterward, the values continuously declined, dropping below zero, and then recovering to 2.5. Equivalent rent values peaked between 1985 and 1986, followed by another peak around 1991, with both reaching above 6.0. Then it suddenly fell in 1991. Between 1991 and 1999, the data fluctuated stably around 3.2. A smaller peak occurred around 2001, with a high point of 4.5, then it fell again, followed by another peak in 2006. Afterward, the values steadily declined, and then recovered to 2.0 by 2012, followed by a slow recovery thereafter. Both lines shared similar change trends in the same period such as 1990 to 1996, and reached peaks at the same time in 1986, 1990, 2002 and 2007.
