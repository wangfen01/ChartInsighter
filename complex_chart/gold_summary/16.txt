This line graph depicts the change in monthly rents for principal residences in the U.S. between January 1984 and February 2015.     The trend was upward from 1985 to 1986, then fell in 1986, and in 1990 it exceeded 6.0, but experienced a sharp drop simultaneously.     Between August 1991 and 1999, the data fluctuated around 3.5.     The data then rose to a smaller peak at the end of 2001, with a peak of 4.5, before falling and then rising again, followed by another peak in 2006.     Thereafter, the value steadily declined, falling to -0.3 in early 2010.     The trend then returned to 2.0 in 2012 and has been slowly recovering since then.     The monthly rents for principal residences in the U.S. were quite volatile.
