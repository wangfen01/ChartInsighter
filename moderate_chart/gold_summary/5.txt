This line chart describes the number of reported cases of acute respiratory infections in the Northern and Southern Hemispheres during different months from 2009 to 2024. The horizontal axis represents time, and the vertical axis represents the number of cases. For the Northern Hemisphere, there is a seasonal trend, typically reaching local peaks in the winter months and local troughs in the summer months, but overall showing a slow upward trend. The Southern Hemisphere also displays a seasonal trend, with local peaks during the winter months and troughs during the summer months. However, overall, the number of acute respiratory infection cases in the Northern Hemisphere is higher than in the Southern Hemisphere.
