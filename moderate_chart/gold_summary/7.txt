This chart shows the change in the average effective retirement age for women in Italy and Mexico from 1994 to 2018.       During this period, the retirement age in Mexico was consistently higher than in Italy, and the gap between the two countries gradually narrowed.       In 1994, Mexican women had a retirement age of about 73 years, which remained stable until 1997, then began to decline, reaching 68 years in 2002, and peaked at 74.7 years in 2007 before showing a downward trend.  From 1994 to 2018, the retirement age in Italy gradually increased from 59.7 to 61.5 years.       There were some fluctuations during this period, with the lowest point at 58.1 years in 1999 and the highest at 61.5 years in 2018.
