The graph represents a line graph of Afghanistan's GDP over time from 2008 to 2022. From 2008 to 2011, Afghanistan's GDP showed a general upward trend from $10.30 billion to $20.29 billion, followed by a more stable change, reaching a peak of $20.62 billion in 2014. From 2014 to 2016, GDP showed a downward trend, with the GDP dropping to $18.02 billion, followed by a slow recovery trend, a recovery that is short-lived and begins in 2021 with a sharp decline in GDP, plummeting to $14.29 billion, the lowest point in the entire time span. In 2022, GDP recovers slightly to $14.47 billion but is still considerably lower than the peak in 2014.
